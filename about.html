Travel Tales

Embracing the Unknown: My Adventure in Phnom Penh

On one of my trips to Southeast Asia, I was exploring in Phnom Penh when I found myself in a situation where I had to navigate a busy market with hardly any knowledge of the local language. It was overwhelming at first, but I quickly realized that the best way to get by was to immerse myself in the culture and embrace the adventure. By the end of the day, I had learned a few key phrases in the local language, tried some new foods, and even made some new friends. The challenges of traveling can often lead to unexpected rewards, like discovering a newfound sense of confidence and independence.

Lost in Gdańsk

During my first ever backpacking trip through Europe, I had a bit of a misadventure when I accidentally got on the wrong train and ended up in a completely different place than I intended - Gdańsk, Poland. At first, I was frustrated and nervous about what was to come, but I quickly realized that this unexpected detour was actually an opportunity to explore a new place and have a unique experience. I spent the day exploring the town and meeting locals, and I even ended up extending my trip to spend more time there. The rewards of travel often come from embracing the unexpected and being open to new experiences!

Milk, Please!

My first trip for 2023 was a return trip to one of my favorite places... Montego Bay, Jamaica, I decided to take a chance and try some of the local cuisine. I ordered a dish that I couldn't pronounce, but the menu described it as a "spicy Jamaican-style chicken taco." When in Rome, right? When the dish arrived, it looked and smelled delicious. I eagerly took a big bite out of the first taco, only to immediately regret my decision. The dish was so spicy that it felt like my mouth was on fire! I quickly downed my drink and asked the waiter for some milk to help soothe the burning sensation. As I sat there sweating and gasping for air, the waiter came over and asked how I was enjoying the dish. I managed to choke out a response between gulps of milk: "It's great, but I think I need a fire extinguisher for my mouth!" The waiter laughed and brought me a milder dish, and I learned a valuable lesson about always asking about the spiciness level of a dish before ordering it in a foreign country.

Things to Keep in Mind When Traveling Somewhere New
DO DON'T
Do your research on the culture and customs of the place you're visiting Don't assume that everyone speaks your language or shares your customs
Try the local cuisine with an open mind and don't be afraid to ask questions Don't be disrespectful of the local cuisine or dietary restrictions
Pack appropriate clothing and gear for the climate and activities Don't wear clothing that is inappropriate or offensive to the local culture
Be respectful of religious and cultural practices Don't make fun of or disrespect local religious or cultural practices
Be aware of your surroundings and safety Don't put yourself in dangerous or risky situations
Be open to new experiences and meeting new people - you'll be suprised! Don't be close-minded or disrespectful to those with different beliefs or backgrounds
Be mindful of your impact on the environment and local communities Don't contribute to pollution or disrespect the local environment or communities
Learn some basic phrases in the local language before your travel Don't assume rely solely on translation apps
Be respectful of local traditions. This also means immersing yourself and appreciating the experience! Don't engage in activities that are illegal or disrespectful to local customs and traditions
Have fun and enjoy the experience! Don't let negative experiences or attitudes ruin your trip
